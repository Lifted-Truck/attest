How a cited reference numeral surfaces its drawing in the main evidence view: the two-signal selection rule, and why no confidence floor is applied.

Figures in the document view — the payoff

Click an answer about "the separator 10" and its drawing appears beside the highlighted text. This is where the whole figures arc — text model, fetch, OCR, mapping — cashes out.

The whole chain, now closed. The spec text names a part by numeral ("necked portion 89"). The deterministic parser knows numeral 89 is recited. The frozen OCR manifest knows 89 sits on sheet p.8. The FIG→sheet map knows p.8 is FIG. 5. So when an answer cites a span containing "89", the evidence view shows FIG. 5's drawing in the document pane — automatically, deterministically, and marked as located by OCR.

1 · The selection rule — two signals, union'd

All of this is a pure function (figures_map.relevant_figures) over the cited text + the frozen manifest — same inputs, same panels (I6). It is display-only: nothing here passes through verify, and a spurious panel costs a glance, never a wrong citation (D21).

2 · The judgment call: no confidence floor

The tempting mistake I didn't make. OCR reads the flagship numeral 89 as "89-" at confidence 0.3 — the leader line (the little arrow from the number to the part) fuses with the digit and drags the score down. My first instinct was to floor low-confidence sightings as noise. But that instinct is backwards here: on a patent drawing, almost every real reference numeral has a leader line, so the low-confidence reads are the real numerals, not the noise. A 0.5 floor would have deleted 89→FIG. 5 — the exact payoff — while removing almost nothing spurious.
What I did instead. No floor on recall; surface the OCR provenance on every panel instead ("located by OCR, conf 0.5" in the caption). A reviewer sees the drawing and how confidently it was placed, and the dedicated figures view (patent_figures_view.py) carries the full per-numeral confidence detail. This is the same lesson as the ≥10-floor bug and the denial-cue census, a third time: a filter that looks like it removes noise can be quietly deleting the signal — so measure what it discards before trusting it.

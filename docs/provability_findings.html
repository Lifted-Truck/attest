Can the “$2,000,000 refutation” be caught — deterministically, at runtime?

Findings of the 51-agent research swarm (8 literature lenses · 42 candidate techniques · hostile review · full synthesis in provability_research.md).

The verdict. In general: no — and the swarm can say precisely why. Whether a figure is asserted or mentioned-and-denied is a pragmatic property of a speaker, not a formal property of the text; a refutation with no surface cue leaves nothing for a deterministic method to see. For exactly one slice: yes. When the denial is lexically marked and sits next to the cited figure ("…claimed $2M, but in fact this is incorrect"), a closed cue-list scan can deterministically force a downgrade to partial/abstain. That slice — and only that slice — can become a hard Layer-0 guarantee. It is one-directional: it can only add abstentions, never permit a present, so it can't create the confident-wrong answer, and it's contract-legal as a monotonic strengthening.

The load-bearing honesty note (the synthesis's own words, sharpened): the $2,000,000 sentence is synthetic — nothing shaped like it occurs in either golden corpus. The actual false-premise trap in the golden set ("did total assets decline?") is refuted by arithmetic in a table row, which no negation/veridicality machinery touches. That's why the staircase starts with a measurement: before spending a gate on lexically-marked refutation, find out whether it exists in real filings and patents at any meaningful rate.

2 · The funnel — 42 candidates in, zero clean survivors

Recurring kill-reasons, worth knowing by name: a model on the deciding path (breaks I6 — the most common); fails in the forbidden direction (passes a bad answer rather than blocking a good one — e.g. NLI's documented numeric blind spot, ~43% of its false positives, landing exactly on ATTEST's flagship class); marginal ≠ per-answer (conformal's guarantee averages over a population and is provably silent about your one catastrophic query); benchmark-to-real collapse (10-word clean-parse sentences vs. a 300-page filing).

3 · The staircase — what ATTEST could actually build

Why the one-directionality matters so much: Rung 2 can only move outcomes toward abstention. It can wrongly block a good answer (recoverable, and measured by Rung 1 before we ship it); it can never wrongly pass a bad one. That's the same precision-over-recall trade the coverage gate made in run 2 — and it's what makes it a legal monotonic strengthening under the truth contract rather than a redesign.

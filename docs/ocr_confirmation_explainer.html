How ATTEST uses the specification text to guide a second, higher-recall OCR pass that recovers reference numerals the first pass missed on patent drawings, illustrated with the separator-10 / FIG-4 case.

Pushing the truth from both angles — text-guided OCR

When two evidence sources disagree, use each to interrogate the other. The spec text said FIG. 4 contains reference numeral 10; OCR didn't find it. So we asked OCR to look again — where the text told it to.

The case that motivated it (spotted on review). FIG. 4 is captioned "a disassembled separator", and the separator is reference numeral 10. The drawing plainly labels it — 10 → at the middle-left. But the first OCR pass read only 80, 82, 83, 86 on that sheet and missed the 10 (along with 84, 88, 95, 90, 52). One evidence source (text) predicted a numeral another (the drawing OCR) failed to confirm. That disagreement is a signal, not just an error.

2 · Why a second look works — tiling

Vision's whole-image OCR downsamples a 2320×3408 scan, so small isolated numerals on sparse line-art get dropped. Re-OCR the sheet in overlapping full-resolution tiles and the small numbers reappear. The prototype (before building anything) confirmed it:

PassRecovered of the 6 missing on FIG. 4
whole image, lowered text-heightnone
tiled (4×2, full-res)10, 52, 84, 90, 95 — 5 of 6
The text prediction is what makes higher recall safe. Tiling alone also surfaces junk (page numbers, patent-number fragments like 199/447/630). Because we search only for the numeral the text predicts on that specific sheet, the noise is ignored by construction — we accept a recovered "10" because the text already told us a "10" should be there. Two independent sources agreeing is the bar.

3 · What it does and doesn't change

PropertyStatus
Determinism on the evidence path (I6)unchanged — this is an ingestion-time pass; its output is frozen + hashed into the manifest, exactly like the first pass (D28). Runtime never re-OCRs.
Provenance honestyrecovered numerals carry method: "text-guided" and render as ↻ p.N — visibly distinct from a first-pass read, at their (lower) OCR confidence.
A drawing is displayed evidence, not a citation (D21)unchanged — grounding still binds claims to the text. OCR, first or second pass, only helps a human find the part.
The unrecoverable casea numeral even the guided pass can't find stays flagged — now a stronger signal (two passes tried), routed to human review, never silently filled.
One bug caught in the build, worth noting. The recovered box first landed mirrored vertically — the tile's rectangle uses a top-left origin while Vision's bounding box is bottom-left, so the y-axis needed flipping back into the full image's frame. Fixed and verified: the box now lands dead-on the 10 →. The confirmation box is itself a check — a box on the wrong mark would expose a bad recovery at a glance.
The principle, stated plainly. No single evidence source is trusted to be complete. The text and the drawing each get to interrogate the other, and where they disagree, the disagreement drives a targeted second look rather than a silent choice. Recovery is confirmed from two angles; an unresolved conflict is surfaced, not hidden. That is the same discipline as the whole system — ground or abstain, never invent — applied to the seam between a patent's words and its drawings.

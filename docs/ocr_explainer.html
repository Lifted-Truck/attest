How OCR joined the system — without breaking determinism

A pre-trained OCR is a model. ATTEST's evidence path forbids models. This is the design that gets both: near-human reading of the 1995 drawing sheets, and a runtime that stays deterministic (D28).

The move in one sentence. OCR runs once, at ingestion — like content-hashing the corpus — and its entire output is frozen into a hashed manifest; everything at runtime and in CI is a pure function over that manifest. Non-determinism is quarantined at the moment of ingestion, where a human can review it, instead of living on the evidence path, where it would silently vary.

3 · The mapping rules — how a garble can't become a fact

Rule 1 — an OCR label only counts if it names a figure the TEXT already knows. The text side (deterministic parsing) says the figures are 1, 2, 3A, 3B, 3C, 4, 5, 6. So when OCR reads "FIG.A", it cannot invent a "figure A" — the label is simply not accepted.

Rule 2 — single-gap elimination, flagged. After Rule 1, exactly one known figure (4) had no sheet and exactly one sheet (page 7) had no accepted label — so they pair by elimination, and the mapping records method: "elimination" with no fake confidence number. Two gaps → nothing is guessed; the gap is surfaced instead.

Result on the real patent: 8/8 figures mapped (7 by OCR, 1 by flagged elimination), and 41 of 62 text-side numerals located on their sheets.

4 · Two traps caught on the way (worth remembering)

Trap 1 — "FIGS. 3 A-C". This patent's own caption style puts a space inside the label. The old reference parser read it as a phantom bare figure "3", which occupied a slot and silently blocked the FIG-4 elimination. Fix: letter-ranges expand deterministically (3 A-C → 3A, 3B, 3C), with a regression test.

Trap 2 — dimension labels. FIG. 6 annotates dimensions D1…D6. Naive digit extraction turned those into "numerals 1–6" — which then falsely "matched" the greywater sources (bathtub 1, toilet 2…) onto the wrong sheet. Fix: a digit run immediately preceded by a letter is a label, not a numeral. Both are the same species as the ≥10-floor bug you caught earlier: plausible extraction rules silently manufacturing or deleting evidence — and both are now pinned by tests.

5 · What it unlocked — PE-2's hardest check, at honest precision

The check that was impossible to do honestly from text alone became a high-precision review list the moment both sides were grounded — the spec's numerals from deterministic parsing, the sheets' from the frozen OCR manifest. It renders in the figures view as a "Structural review" section.

6 · The honesty story (your condition, kept)

OCR is not 100% reliable, and the system says so everywhere it appears: the manifest embeds a warning block and per-observation confidence; every OCR-derived tag in the UI reads "located by OCR" (never "cited"); elimination-assigned figures carry an asterisk; and 21 unlocated numerals are presented as exactly that — not located, with OCR-miss and drawing-omission explicitly indistinguishable. A drawing remains displayed evidence (D21): grounding still binds every claim to the text that recites a numeral. Nothing OCR produces can pass through verify.

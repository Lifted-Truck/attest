# ATTEST — Landscape Lessons (external prior art, mapped to current state)

**Status:** research input, 2026-07-05. **Subordinate to `ROADMAP.md`** (single source of truth for sequencing) and `ATTEST_build_brief.md` (design). Nothing in this document authorizes starting work. Items marked **CANDIDATE** are proposals routed to ROADMAP triage — the human decides whether they become tasks, decisions, or backlog. Items marked **CONFIRMS** require no action; they are external validation of an existing decision and are recorded so the agent does not "fix" what the market has already proven correct.

**Why this document exists.** Services claiming ATTEST-adjacent functionality ("hallucination-free," "every claim traceable," "grounded answers") now have a multi-year public record in law and finance — including a preregistered academic falsification of vendor claims, a ~1,400-case database of court-documented failures, escalating sanctions, and a new product category (citation-verification layers) born directly from those failures. This maps their mistakes and successes onto ATTEST's invariants (I1–I6), decisions (D1–D22), and truth contract (v1.1).

## §1 · The falsified claim: "hallucination-free" defined too narrowly

**External record.** The Stanford preregistered evaluation (Magesh, Surani, Dahl, Suzgun, Manning, Ho — *Journal of Empirical Legal Studies*, 2025; arXiv:2405.20362) tested LexisNexis Lexis+ AI and Thomson Reuters Westlaw AI-AR / Ask Practical Law after vendors marketed RAG as "eliminating" (Casetext), "avoid[ing]" (Thomson Reuters), or guaranteeing "hallucination-free" citations (LexisNexis). Measured hallucination rates: **17–33%**. The study's central analytic move is a two-dimensional typology — **correctness** (factual accuracy) vs **groundedness** (does the cited source actually support the claim). Its sharpest finding: LexisNexis's claim was true only in the narrowest sense — links resolved to *real documents*; whether the document was relevant, or even contradicted the claim, was outside the guarantee. A response can be correct but misgrounded (poor retrieval + lucky answer + false attribution), which the authors flag as the most dangerous failure because it misleads a verifying reader. LexisNexis subsequently retreated to "our promise is not perfection, but that all linked legal citations are hallucination-free."

**Mapping to ATTEST.**

- **CONFIRMS — the `verified ≠ entailed` declared non-guarantee (truth contract v1.x; README "What v1 actually guarantees").** ATTEST states in one sentence exactly the distinction that took a preregistered study to force out of the incumbents: verified means *exists, verbatim, uniquely-resolvable, hash-matched* — not *supports*. This is the single most defensible line in the repo. Do not soften it, do not let marketing copy blur it, and when the pitch is written, cite the Stanford typology as the independent vocabulary for why the split is honest (their "groundedness" = our entailment; their narrow "hallucination-free" = precisely the overclaim we refuse).
- **CONFIRMS — D13 (question frame + constraint coverage).** The correct-but-misgrounded failure is the exact case D13 attacks at runtime: coverage converts a chunk of entailment from offline-judged into deterministic checkable ("the cited span carries the question's subject + attribute + qualifiers"). The documented residuals (negation, attachment, coreference → Layer-E) match the study's observation that groundedness failures are subtler than existence failures. The M2-T8 remainder (live agent emits the frame; present-or-abstain requires `verify.ok AND coverage.complete`) is, per this record, the highest-leverage open item in the shared engine — it is the mechanism that separates ATTEST's guarantee from the one that got falsified.
- **CONFIRMS — D4 (hallucination gate = literal 0, ε as adjudication process, not tolerance).** The incumbents' error was claiming zero while shipping 17–33%. ATTEST's construction — CI hard-fails on any non-adjudicated judge flag — is the only shape under which a zero claim survives an external audit, because the adjudication log *is* the audit response.

**Positioning note (no code).** Every public artifact (README already complies) should state the guarantee in the falsifiable form and never in the vendor form. The market has demonstrated the cost of the alternative: the phrase "hallucination-free" is now a liability that invites the Stanford treatment.

## §2 · The failure record: taxonomy and scale as market data

**External record.** Damien Charlotin's public AI Hallucination Cases Database (HEC Paris) tracks ~1,400+ court decisions worldwide (1,000+ US) where tribunals addressed AI-fabricated or misgrounded material; growth ran 5–6 new cases/day in early 2026 before plateauing around 350–400/quarter. Sanctions escalated from Mata v. Avianca's $5,000 (2023) to $15,000/attorney at the Sixth Circuit, a $110,000 Oregon order (23 fabricated citations, 8 invented quotations, case dismissed with prejudice), bar suspensions, and fee-shifting to opposing counsel. Two structural points: (1) the database **classifies incidents into categories requiring different detection strategies** — fabricated authority, real-authority-misrepresented, fabricated quotation within real authority, etc.; (2) Charlotin then built an automated checker (**PelAIkan**) *from* the failure corpus — dataset first, tool second. Commentary has converged on the architectural framing: the model cannot verify that a source exists, says what is claimed, or remains current — "the gap is architectural, not a capability problem," and "using one AI to check another AI's output does not constitute independent verification."

**Mapping to ATTEST.**

- **CONFIRMS — I6 / Layer-0 no-model-calls; D6 runtime split.** The "AI checking AI is not verification" critique is answered structurally: the checker is a pure deterministic function; the only model-as-judge sits quarantined in Layer-E with human adjudication (D4). This is the argument to lead with when a prospect asks "but doesn't your verifier hallucinate too?"
- **CONFIRMS — golden-set-first build order (M0 audition rig → everything else).** PelAIkan independently replicated ATTEST's sequence: the labeled failure corpus precedes and generates the checker.
- **CANDIDATE (triage) — a named failure taxonomy for golden negatives.** ATTEST's outcome classes (D16/D22: answer/abstain/correction/partial/refuse) classify *expected behavior*. The Charlotin corpus suggests a complementary axis: classifying *planted failure modes* the oracle must catch — fabricated source (no resolution), real-source-misquoted (verbatim mismatch → I3/D9 catches), real-source-misgrounded (resolves + hash-matches but wrong metric/period/entity → D13 coverage or Layer-E), fabricated quotation inside a real span, derived-value miscomputation (D18/D19 catches), stale-source (I3 hash drift). Several already have standing tests (planted-unbound, wrong-offset, wrong-derived in `tests/test_verify.py`; the naive-citation coverage card). The proposal is only to (a) name the taxonomy in one doc section, (b) audit the golden sets for at least one item per mode, and (c) tag existing negatives with their mode so Layer-E can report per-mode catch rates. Cheap, mostly bookkeeping, and it turns "our oracle is thorough" into an enumerable claim. Natural home: a short section in the brief + tags in `golden_seed.json` / `golden_patent.json`; possibly a PE-5 sub-item.

## §3 · The verification layer as product category — and the audit trail as the deliverable

**External record.** The sanctions wave created a product category that barely existed in 2023. **Clearbrief** (Word add-in; AmLaw 200, courts, Microsoft Legal) detects every citation to facts *and* law, resolves each to source, and — the distinctive move — computes a **per-sentence semantic support score** ("patented semantic analysis score that compares each sentence to its cited source and flags low scores where the source does not appear to support the assertion"), plus quote-accuracy checks. Their checker deliberately uses "classic AI rather than generative AI... no risk of the checking tool itself introducing hallucinations." In Dec 2025 they shipped the **Cite Check Report**: a generated audit trail proving every citation was systematically reviewed — flagged issues, who resolved each, reviewer notes explaining ignored flags — built explicitly because "partners are being sanctioned for citation errors they didn't personally make" and courts demand documented proof the ethical duty was discharged. **BriefCatch RealityCheck** (Legalweek 2026) launched with a two-layer design: deterministic citation validation (reporter volumes, court identifiers) + a second analysis layer. Charlotin's PelAIkan rounds out the category.

**Mapping to ATTEST.**

- **CONFIRMS — the entire two-layer architecture, independently and commercially replicated.** Deterministic resolution layer + scored support layer + audit artifact is now the convergent design of three shipping products. ATTEST arrived at the same shape from first principles (Layer-0 / Layer-E / audit log). No action; record for the pitch: "the category leaders converged on our architecture; we differ in making the deterministic layer a *runtime guarantee* rather than a post-hoc check."
- **CONFIRMS — `check_claim` (D3).** "Paste your sentence, see if your docs back it" is not just a demo — it is the *category*. Clearbrief's core loop (claim → located support → strength flag) over an opponent's filed brief is `check_claim` over a third-party document. The sanctions market demonstrates independent demand for verification of *someone else's* AI output, decoupled from ATTEST's own answer pipeline. Positioning, not code: `check_claim` is a standalone product surface, and D3's "this is the one cut if leanest-ship is needed" should be re-weighted in light of it being the fastest-growing demand signal in the record.
- **CANDIDATE (triage) — graded support score as a middle layer between binary coverage and the Layer-E judge.** Today the support signal is three-valued in practice: deterministic coverage complete/incomplete (D13), plus an offline binary entailment verdict (Layer-E judge). Clearbrief's per-sentence *scored* support (their retrieval-similarity between sentence and cited page) is a cheap, model-free-or-frozen-model, deterministic-per-version signal that sits between the two: not a guarantee (never gate on it — that would smuggle a soft judgment into the evidence path and violate the D12/D21 discipline), but an **advisory rigor line** in the evidence view and a Layer-E feature (does low score predict judge non-entailment? does it predict the agent's over-claims from LE-2?). Fits the D21 upgrade ratchet exactly: adopt behind a seam, ship only if Layer-0 holds and Layer-E improves-or-holds; and it is a plausible first concrete `EntailmentProvider` shape, which per TC-3 gets an interface only when a *second* implementation (e.g., the v2 inline entailment gate) exists. Explicitly **not** a v1 gate; explicitly not runtime; triage into Backlog or a Layer-E experiment alongside the query-relative floor experiment (D20's revisit clause).
- **CANDIDATE (triage) — the Review Report: package the audit trail as the client-facing deliverable.** ATTEST already has the substrate no competitor matches: hash-chained append-only log (M3), byte-identical replay, provenance stamping with contract/method versions (TC-2), and the evidence view. What it does not yet have is the *document a professional signs*: a generated report for an engagement/session stating — corpus identity (doc ids + content hashes), every interaction with outcome class, every verify record with its atom bindings and recomputed derivations, every flag and its disposition, every human adjudication (the D4 log), the truth-contract version and declared non-guarantees, and a place for reviewer notes. Clearbrief's insight is that in a liability regime (ABA Formal Opinion 512: the verification duty stays with the lawyer; 35+ state bars now require verification), **the proof-of-process artifact is what the client is buying** — the located evidence is necessary but the signed trail is the deliverable. For the patent engagement this is nearly forced: the professional-in-the-loop (D10) needs exactly this to discharge their own duty. Natural home: the RT track (it composes RT-1 session history + the evidence view + the audit chain into one export; RT-1 is its prerequisite), with the patent deliverable format question (intake Q13/Q16) as the forcing client input. Mostly assembly of existing verified parts; the one new design decision is the human-adjudication/notes record (which D22's "revisit when" already anticipates for refusal logging — same record shape).

## §4 · Abstention economics: the benchmark that punished honesty

**External record.** In the Vals Legal AI Report (Feb 2025; ten AmLaw firms supplying real tasks; Harvey, CoCounsel, Vincent AI, Oliver evaluated; Lexis+AI withdrew mid-study), vLex's Vincent AI **refuses to answer when it judges the data insufficient — and this conservative design "negatively affected benchmark scores compared to tools attempting answers despite uncertainty."** The scoring rewarded attempted answers. Meanwhile the Oct 2025 follow-up weighted Accuracy 50% / Authoritativeness 40% / Appropriateness 10% — still no credit axis for calibrated refusal. Separately: vendor opt-in per task means published leaderboards carry survivorship bias (Lexis withdrew from the sections where it was evaluated).

**Mapping to ATTEST.**

- **CONFIRMS — D16 + D22 (five outcome classes; `refusal_accuracy` scored separately; refusals no longer dilute abstention) and the Brier/reliability calibration metric.** ATTEST's eval already refuses to make Vincent's-benchmark's mistake against itself: abstention-when-unsupported, grounded correction, and refuse-to-adjudicate are *success* classes with their own rates. The external record shows why this is rare and valuable — the README's "the metric almost no one measures" line is literally true in the commercial benchmarks. Guard this: any future scoring change that would let presented-answer rates dominate the headline metric recreates the Vincent trap and should be treated as a weakening under D21's monotonic rule (major bump + rationale).
- **CONFIRMS — refusal *quality* follow-up (PE-5 remaining: refuse **and** offer located evidence; 3/6 refused without locating).** Vincent's benchmark penalty came from refusing *emptily*. ATTEST's differentiator is D22's construction — the evidence is often present; what is declined is the conclusion, while the located evidence is offered for professional review. The PE-5 remaining item is therefore not polish; it is the exact behavior that converts the market's "conservative tools score worse" problem into a strength.
- **CONFIRMS — public, preregistered-style golden sets and published trend files.** Closed systems resisted evaluation until Stanford forced it; the vendor that withdrew mid-benchmark took reputational damage anyway. ATTEST's golden sets, gate tables, and baseline docs living in the open repo are the credibility posture the incumbents lack. Keep Layer-E trend files (`layer_e_results.jsonl`, baseline docs) committed and public for the reference build.

## §5 · The finance parallel: declared determinism boundaries

**External record.** Finance converged on ATTEST's architecture independently. **Kepler** ("AI that proves it's right"): AI handles question complexity, deterministic code handles data; every number sourced to filing/page/line-item; no probabilities — same input, same output. **Finrep**: "every response reproducible, reviewable, and attributable to underlying source disclosures... no stochastic reasoning in regulated workflows," built directly on EDGAR primary sources. **V7**: every extracted datum visually linked to its source location for audit trails. And the institutional trust criteria, stated by buy-side practitioners: verifiable live track records (not backtests), full source citations tracing every claim to a specific section of a specific filing, and **"honesty about which parts of the pipeline are deterministic and which rely on probabilistic AI."**

**Mapping to ATTEST.**

- **CONFIRMS — D1 (EDGAR reference corpus).** The reference build sits in a domain where multiple funded companies are proving demand for exactly this guarantee. The demo is legible to a real buyer class without translation.
- **CONFIRMS — truth contract (D21/TC-1) as the declared deterministic/probabilistic boundary.** The buy-side criterion is satisfied *by an artifact ATTEST already ships*: every guarantee → mechanism → layer → strength, with the runtime/Layer-0/Layer-E split marking precisely which parts are deterministic. One positioning refinement: the truth contract is currently written for the repo; a one-page client-facing rendering of the same table (which could be a section of the §3 Review Report) answers the institutional question verbatim.
- **CONFIRMS — TC-2 provenance stamping.** Kepler's "click any figure to see where it came from" is the evidence view; ATTEST's addition — stamping *which contract version and which methods* produced/checked each record — exceeds anything in the surveyed record and should be named in the pitch as such.

## §6 · Distribution and surface lessons

**External record.** Clearbrief lives inside Microsoft Word at the moment of signing; reviewers cite this as why it gets used ("works where lawyers already do"). Harvey's pitch is "access Harvey where you already work." **Hebbia's** distinctive surface is the Matrix grid: each prompt decomposed into subtasks run in parallel across the document set, results in a spreadsheet-style grid where every cell's citation points back to document text — reviewers note this scales better than chat-RAG on large corpora and gives professionals "a familiar review surface, not a chat thread." Harvey topped the Vals document-Q&A task (94.8%) on the strength of ingestion/chunking/retrieval, while all tools *underperformed lawyers* on EDGAR research and redlining — retrieval over messy structured filings remains the weak joint even for the best-funded player.

**Mapping to ATTEST.**

- **CONFIRMS — D17 (desktop-first; defer M5).** The market's distribution lesson — live at the point of work, not in a destination app — is D17's rationale verbatim. The React replay app remains correctly deferred until a client deliverable needs it.
- **CONFIRMS — structure-aware ingestion investments (D14; PE-1 document model).** The strongest commercial system still loses to humans on EDGAR-style structured-document research; ATTEST's split-date normalization finding (D14) and the paragraph/claim/limitation addressability work (PE-1) are aimed at exactly the industry-wide weak joint. The G009 lexical-gap recall miss and the retrieval-upgrade seam (M1-T3 deferral, strengthened by PE-5's present/absent overlap finding) are the shared-engine items this record says to keep prioritized.
- **CANDIDATE (triage, low weight) — grid rendering for multi-item review.** PE-3's per-limitation support mapping is structurally Hebbia's Matrix row (item × evidence × verdict). The current per-claim two-pane view (`patent_support_view.py`) is the right primitive; if an engagement ever needs *portfolio-* or *all-claims-* scale review, a grid (rows = limitations/claims, columns = support status/edges/gaps, cells → click-to-span) is the surface the market has validated for that scale. Note only; no v1 need.

## §7 · Consolidated: what changed, what didn't

**Nothing in the external record contradicts a standing decision.** The survey independently validates D3, D4, D6, D13, D16, D17, D21, D22, I3–I6, the golden-first build order, and the deliberate `verified ≠ entailed` line. Where the incumbents failed, ATTEST's corresponding decision already exists and is tested.

**Three candidates for human triage** (do not start; route per ROADMAP working agreements):

1. **Failure-mode taxonomy tags on golden negatives** (§2) — bookkeeping-scale; enables per-mode catch-rate reporting; plausible PE-5/brief addition.
2. **Review Report deliverable** (§3) — assembles existing verified substrates (audit chain, replay, provenance stamps, evidence view, adjudication notes) into the signable proof-of-process artifact the liability regime is creating demand for; RT-track; RT-1 is prerequisite; patent intake Q13/Q16 is the forcing input.
3. **Graded support score, advisory-only** (§3) — Layer-E experiment / Backlog; never a runtime gate; candidate first shape for the eventual `EntailmentProvider` seam (TC-3).

**Two positioning notes** (docs/pitch, no engine work): state the guarantee only in the falsifiable form and cite the Stanford typology as independent vocabulary (§1); render the truth contract's deterministic/probabilistic boundary as a client-facing page, matching the institutional trust criterion verbatim (§5).
